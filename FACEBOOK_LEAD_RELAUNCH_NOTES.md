## Facebook Lead Relaunch Notes

Date checked: April 8, 2026

### Campaign reviewed

- `Denali | Network Consultation | FB | Mar 2026`

### Actual Meta results

- Status: `Off`
- Spend: `$125.08`
- Impressions: `16,143`
- Landing page views: `911`
- Cost per landing page view: `$0.14`
- Budget: `$10/day`

### Diagnosis

The campaign delivered inexpensive traffic, but it was optimized for `Landing Page Views`, not `Leads`.

That means Meta learned to find cheap visitors instead of people likely to submit a form.

The landing page also had too much friction for cold Facebook traffic:

- abstract headline
- form asked for too much
- weak immediate offer
- captcha added extra friction

### Page changes prepared locally

- stronger headline focused on Wi-Fi problems
- clearer proof and local trust cues
- simplified CTA language
- lower-friction form
- email no longer required unless user wants email contact
- budget and home-size fields removed

### Do before relaunch

1. Change campaign optimization to `Leads`
2. Optimize for the `Lead` event, not landing page views
3. Confirm Pixel lead event is received in Meta Events Manager
4. Submit one real test lead end to end
5. Confirm email delivery to `services@denalitechs.com`
6. Keep budget small during the next test

### Suggested next campaign structure

- Objective: `Leads`
- Conversion location: `Website`
- Optimization event: `Lead`
- Geography: Chicago service area only
- Audience:
  - one broad ad set
  - one homeowner / smart-home interest ad set
- Creative:
  - stronger problem-based headline
  - clear CTA: `Book My Wi-Fi Consultation`

### Success threshold for next test

If the next campaign gets traffic but still produces zero leads after a meaningful sample, the next issue is likely:

- weak offer
- poor audience targeting
- low trust on the page
- broken lead delivery path
