Why this page exists

Homeowners need to know how Denali chooses gear

This page is not here to throw logos at you. It is here to explain how Denali protects homeowners from thin ecosystems, unsupported products, and systems that become harder to live with after the install is over.

Company standard

  • We do not sell product lines we cannot support after install.
  • We choose brands with proven support paths, not short-lived ecosystems.
  • The standard is homeowner usability first, not the longest gear list.
  • Infrastructure brands matter just as much as the shiny room-facing brands.

Product context

The useful brand story is what the equipment changes in the house

Power recovery, rack serviceability, network stability, wiring quality, and video distribution are not glamorous. They are still part of why a system feels better to own.

WattBox and OvrC

Strong racks and structured wiring

Clean racks make theater, networking, audio, and camera systems easier to support.

Binary video paths

Video distribution depends on choosing the right cable, extender, or AV-over-IP path.

What this protects against

Cheaper today can become more expensive to live with later
