Fast answers

The questions that usually matter before a homeowner reaches out

These answers are meant to reduce guesswork. They are not a sales script and they are not here to make every house sound the same.

Do you help with existing systems or only new projects?

Both. Denali Tech helps with inherited smart-home systems, Control4 or URC cleanup, WiFi problems, rack issues, and practical upgrades as well as new installs.

What areas do you serve?

Denali Tech is based in Mount Prospect and works across Chicago and nearby suburbs including Arlington Heights, Schaumburg, Elk Grove Village, Des Plaines, Park Ridge, Niles, Skokie, Evanston, Glenview, and Northbrook.

Which systems do you usually work on?

The main categories are smart-home control, WiFi and networking, cameras and doorbells, lighting and shades, home theater, audio, rack planning, and structured wiring.

Do you offer project reviews or consultations?

Yes. The first step is usually a planning call, a service visit, or a project review based on the room, system, city, and what feels wrong right now.

How does pricing usually work?

Pricing depends on scope, product path, labor, and how much of the current system can stay. Denali Tech would rather define the right scope first than throw out a fast number that does not hold up later.

How long does installation take?

That depends on the size of the job. A focused service or network cleanup can be much shorter than a multi-room lighting, theater, or whole-home automation project. Denali Tech sets the timeline after the scope is clear.

Do you support what you install after the job is done?

Yes. Denali Tech stays available after installation for support, changes, and follow-through. The company standard is to use product lines that can still be supported instead of short-lived gear.

Can the system stay simple for the homeowner to use?

That is the goal. Denali Tech is not trying to make the house feel more technical. Good planning should reduce app clutter, remote confusion, and daily friction.

What brands do you usually work with?

The stack depends on the job, but the main supported lines include Control4, URC, Lutron, Sonos, Araknis, Luma, WattBox, OvrC, Strong, and other proven dealer-supported products.

What should I send in the first message?

Send the room or system, the city, what feels off, and a few photos if possible. That is usually enough to decide whether the next step is planning, service, or a larger install conversation.
